How to Play Battleship

  • Before the game begins, each player can place their 5 ships on their grid. The other player will not know where your ships are being placed, and vice versa.
  • Both players have the same ships and are listed along with their sizes:
    1. Carrier (5)
    2. Battleship (4)
    3. Destroyer (3)
    4. Submarine (3)
    5. Patrol Boat (2)
  • When placing ships, keep in mind their size as that is how many grid squares they will take up. Also, ships cannot overlap on your grid.
  • Ships can be placed horizontally or vertically, but not diagonally.
  • Once each player has their ships set, the game can begin.
  • During each turn, the player can send a torpedo to a grid square in hope of landing a shot on the hidden enemy ship. If a ship is hit, the player can continue to send torpedos. If the shot is missed, then the turn ends.
  • There are 2 super powers that players can use:
    • Send 3 torpedos in one turn (instead of just 1)
    • Send a big torpedo that will hit 5 grid squares (in the shape of a +)
  • A super power can only be used if a player only has at most 2 ships remaining.
  • Only one super power can be used by each player in a game.
  • Once a player's ships have been completely sunk, the game ends.
